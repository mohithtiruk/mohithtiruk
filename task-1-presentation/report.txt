To create this visual guide for the "Student Digital Ambassador" program, I used a modern digital illustration tool that allowed for vibrant, neon-style graphics against a dark, high-tech background. The choice of medium was essential to make the resource feel contemporary and engaging for a student audience.

The resulting infographic is structured into three clear horizontal bands that address key aspects of digital citizenship. The top band, in blue, defines Digital Literacy by breaking it down into four core competencies: accessing information, creating content, communicating effectively, and problem-solving. The middle band, in green, identifies practical "Useful Digital Tools," categorising them into research, productivity, collaboration, and learning categories with recognisable software icons. Finally, the bottom purple band covers "Professional Online Presence & Safe Practices," providing advice on personal branding alongside safety measures like data protection and ethical citizenship.

The most challenging, yet rewarding, aspect of this process was achieving a balanced layout that was information-rich without becoming cluttered. Selecting precise icons and keeping the text extremely concise was crucial for ensuring that the visual hierarchy remained strong and that the message was immediately clear.
