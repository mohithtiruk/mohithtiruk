While researching UPI fraud, I was most surprised by how attackers exploit the "User Interface" of payment apps to confuse victims. I previously assumed cybercrime required complex coding or malware, but I realized that social engineering—simply tricking a human into pressing a button—is far more common and effective. It was eye-opening to learn that most financial frauds in India are successful because victims don't realize that a PIN is strictly for "sending" money.

As a result, the habit I will personally change is my approach to Two-Factor Authentication. I used to rely solely on SMS-based codes, but I have now switched to a dedicated Authenticator app. This adds a layer of security that prevents "SIM-swapping" attacks, ensuring my academic and personal data remains secure even if my phone number is compromised.
